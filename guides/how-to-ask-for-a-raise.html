How to Ask for a Raise With Numbers

Prepare for a raise conversation with evidence, timing, and a clean ask.

A raise conversation goes better when you stop talking in vague terms like “I work hard” and start talking in outcomes. Revenue supported, projects delivered, systems improved, risk reduced, and responsibilities expanded are all stronger than general effort language.

Calculate the real impact first

Use the raise calculator before the conversation. If you know what a 5%, 8%, or 12% increase really changes after tax and inflation, your ask becomes sharper.

Bring evidence, not emotion

List wins with concrete impact. Think in terms of money saved, time saved, targets achieved, customer impact, or expanded ownership.

Choose timing carefully

After a visible win, before compensation cycles close, or during role expansion discussions are usually better moments than random stressful weeks.

Know your walk-away threshold

Sometimes the value of the process is clarity. If the numbers do not work, use your new understanding to compare offers and plan your next move.

Frequently asked questions

What if my manager says there is no budget?

Ask about timing, what would justify a future increase, and whether title, scope, or bonus options exist.

Should I mention market salary data?

Yes, but use it as context, not your only argument. Your internal impact story matters more.

What number should I ask for?

Ask with a range or a clear target informed by your calculator and market context.
