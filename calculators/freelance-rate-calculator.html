How to read the result

Your result is a pricing floor, not always the final market price. If your niche, urgency, or expertise is stronger, your real rate can go higher. The key is to avoid quoting below the math required to sustain the business.

Frequently asked questions

Why does the suggested rate feel high?

Because freelancers pay for non-billable time, taxes, software, sales, and admin. A rate that looks high often reflects real business costs.

Can I use this for project pricing?

Yes. Start with the hourly floor, then estimate total hours and add scope risk or revision limits.

What are billable hours?

Billable hours are the hours you can actually charge to clients, not every hour you work in a week.
