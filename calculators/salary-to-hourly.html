Why salary-to-hourly matters

A salary can sound bigger than it feels. Converting it into an hourly number gives you a cleaner way to compare jobs with different schedules and responsibilities.

Use it to compare offers

This page is useful when one offer is salaried and another is hourly or contract-based. Once you know the real hourly number, you can compare the trade-off more fairly.

Do not forget unpaid workload

If a salaried role regularly spills past its expected hours, the real hourly rate can be lower than it first appears. Adjust weekly hours upward if you want a stricter comparison.

Good follow-up question

After getting the number, ask what decision this result should help with next: comparing offers, quoting work, setting a budget, building a fund, or speeding up debt payoff.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use a 37.5-hour week?

Yes. Enter the hours that match your real schedule.

What if I do unpaid overtime?

Increase the weekly hours to approximate the true time commitment.

Is this useful for negotiating?

Yes. It helps you compare the real value of a salary against hourly, contract, or freelance alternatives.
