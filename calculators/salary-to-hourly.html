  • Compare salary offers against freelance rates or hourly roles.

How to use the number well

The calculator divides annual salary by paid weeks and weekly hours to estimate the hourly rate. It also shows monthly and weekly pay for easier budgeting.

What can change the result

It does not include overtime rules, bonuses, commissions, taxes, benefits, or unpaid leave unless you adjust the salary or weeks yourself.

Good follow-up move

Once you have the number, open one related tool and one related guide. That usually turns a single estimate into a better decision.

Why the hourly view changes the decision

Annual salary can hide the real value of your time when schedules, unpaid time off, and expected hours are different across roles. Converting the number can make two similar offers look very different.

Use this page when you need a cleaner comparison between salaried work, contract work, and roles with different weekly hour expectations.

Frequently asked questions

What paid weeks should I use?

Use 52 for a full paid year. Lower it if you expect unpaid leave or seasonal work.

Can I compare salary to contract work with this?

Yes, but contract work often requires adding taxes, benefits, and unpaid admin time. Pair this page with the freelance rate calculator.

Why does effective hourly pay matter?

It gives you a clearer view of what your time is worth across roles with different schedules.
